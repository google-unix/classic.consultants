What are the mandatory requirements for an FSSAI Basic Registration?

FSSAI Basic Registration requires: (1) Aadhaar card / PAN card of proprietor, (2) Passport-size photograph, (3) Address proof of business premises — electricity bill or rental agreement, (4) List of food products to be handled. The annual turnover of your business must be below ₹12 Lakhs to qualify for Basic Registration.

What is the difference between Basic, State, and Central FSSAI Licenses?

Basic Registration: For businesses with turnover below ₹12 Lakhs/year — petty food operators, small hawkers, home bakers.

State License: For businesses with turnover between ₹12 Lakhs and ₹20 Crores, operating within a single state (Karnataka in this case).

Central License: For large businesses with turnover above ₹20 Crores, importers, exporters, or those operating across multiple states.

What are the government fees for applying for FSSAI license?

Government fees (per year):
• Basic Registration: ₹100/year
• State License: ₹2,000–₹5,000/year depending on business category
• Central License: ₹7,500/year

These are in addition to our professional service fees. The government fee is paid directly to FSSAI through the FoSCoS portal.

What is the standard timeline to get the license approved?

Standard timelines after submitting complete documents:
• Basic Registration: 7–10 business days
• State License: 20–30 business days
• Central License: 30–45 business days

With Classic Consultants, we often achieve faster approvals due to our error-free applications and established relationships. Same-day processing initiation is available.

Are home bakers required to have an FSSAI Registration?

Yes! Any entity involved in the manufacture, processing, packaging, storage, transportation, distribution, import, export, or sale of food products — including home bakers selling online or through social media — must obtain FSSAI registration. For most home bakers, a Basic FSSAI Registration (₹100/year) is sufficient as long as annual turnover remains below ₹12 Lakhs.

What are the penalties for operating without an FSSAI License?

Operating without an FSSAI license is a serious offence under the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006. Penalties include:
• Imprisonment up to 6 months AND/OR a fine up to ₹5 Lakhs for operating without a license
• Fines up to ₹10 Lakhs for manufacturing or selling substandard food
• Imprisonment up to life for causing grievous harm through adulterated food

It's strongly advised to get licensed before starting operations.

How do I use the FoSCoS portal for renewal?

The FoSCoS (Food Safety Compliance System) portal at foscos.fssai.gov.in is used for all FSSAI filings including renewal. The renewal process requires:
1. Login to FoSCoS with your existing FSSAI number
2. Submit renewal application 30 days before expiry
3. Upload updated documents if there are any changes
4. Pay the renewal fee online

We recommend letting us handle the renewal to avoid technical issues and ensure timely submission. Contact us 45 days before your license expiry.

Can I modify my existing FSSAI license to add new products?

Yes. You can apply for a license modification to: (1) Add new food categories/products, (2) Change your registered address, (3) Change business name, (4) Add or remove partners/directors. We handle all modifications through the FoSCoS portal. Modifications typically take 15–20 days to process. Contact us for a custom quote.

Do cloud kitchens need FSSAI registration?

Absolutely. Cloud kitchens, ghost kitchens, and virtual restaurants in Bangalore are required to obtain FSSAI registration just like any other food business. Most cloud kitchens qualify for a State License. Platforms like Swiggy and Zomato also require a valid FSSAI number before onboarding. We specialize in fast-tracking cloud kitchen registrations.

What happens if my FSSAI license expires?

If your FSSAI license expires, you are technically operating without a valid license and are subject to the same penalties as operating without a license. You must apply for renewal immediately. There's no grace period under law, though FSSAI may allow late renewal with a penalty. We strongly recommend renewing 30–45 days before expiry to avoid disruption.
